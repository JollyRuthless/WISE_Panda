# Combat Log Glossary

This file is a plain-language reference for the special words and markers that appear in SWTOR combat logs.


## Core Line Shape

Each log line generally looks like this:

```text
[timestamp] [source] [target] [ability] [effect] (result) <threat>
```


## Effect Keywords

These are the main event words found inside the `[effect]` block.

- `Event`
  A general system or state event.

- `ApplyEffect`
  Something was applied to a target.
  This can include damage, healing, buffs, debuffs, dots, hots, and status effects.

- `RemoveEffect`
  Something ended or was removed from a target.

- `Spend`
  A resource was spent.

- `Restore`
  A resource was gained or restored.

- `ModifyCharges`
  A charge or stack count changed.

- `AreaEntered`
  A character entered a zone or area.

- `EnterCombat`
  Combat started.

- `ExitCombat`
  Combat ended.

- `AbilityActivate`
  An ability was activated or pressed.

- `TargetSet`
  A target was selected.

- `TargetCleared`
  A target was cleared.

- `Death`
  An entity died.

- `DisciplineChanged`
  The player changed discipline/spec.


## Effect Detail Keywords

These are usually the words after the `:` inside the effect block.

Examples:

```text
[ApplyEffect {…}: Damage {…}]
[ApplyEffect {…}: Heal {…}]
[Spend {…}: Force {…}]
```

Common meanings:

- `Damage`
  This line deals damage.

- `Heal`
  This line heals.

- Resource names such as:
  - `Force`
  - `rage point`
  - `Ammo`
  - `Energy`

- Buff or debuff names such as:
  - `Sprint`
  - `Kolto Shell`
  - `Stunned (Force)`
  - `Slowed (Physical)`
  - `Marked Target (DPS)`


## Result Keywords

These appear in the `(result)` part after the effect block.

- `*`
  Critical hit or critical heal.

- `~`
  Overheal amount.

- `absorbed`
  Part of the damage was absorbed.

- `reflected`
  The damage was reflected.


## Avoidance and Miss Keywords

These show that damage did not land normally.

- `miss`
- `dodge`
- `parry`
- `deflect`
- `immune`
- `resist`
- `shield`
- `glance`
- blank `-`
  Sometimes a zero-damage blank result is effectively a miss.


## Entity Markers

These appear in the `[source]` and `[target]` blocks.

- `@Name`
  A player.

- `@Name#123...`
  A player with an id.

- `@Name/Companion {...}:...`
  A companion owned by that player.

- `NPC Name {entity_id}:instance`
  An NPC.

- `=`
  The special self marker.

- `[]`
  Empty value.


## Numeric Markers

- `(45)`
  A resource amount, such as spend or restore.

- `(123 kinetic {…})`
  Damage amount plus damage type.

- `(123* ~50)`
  Critical amount with overheal.

- `(6 charges {…})`
  Charge count.

- `<1234>`
  Threat value.


## Quick Reading Guide

Use this left-to-right reading pattern:

1. `[timestamp]`
   When it happened.

2. `[source]`
   Who caused it.

3. `[target]`
   Who received it.

4. `[ability]`
   What ability or action was involved.

5. `[effect]`
   What kind of event happened.

6. `(result)`
   The amount, outcome, crit, miss, absorb, or overheal details.

7. `<threat>`
   Threat generated by the event, if present.


## Non-Combat and System Log Lines

The combat log can also contain lines where no combat happened at all.
These are still useful because they describe location changes, login state,
buff state, spec state, and targeting behavior.

Your sample non-combat log includes these patterns:

- `AreaEntered`
  Shows that the player entered a new area or zone.

  Example meanings:
  - Entered `Imperial Fleet`
  - Entered `Dromund Kaas`

  These lines can also include extra metadata after the effect block, such as:
  - `(he3000)`
  - `<v7.0.0b>`

  Those appear to be client or world/build metadata rather than combat data.

- `DisciplineChanged`
  Shows the player’s class and discipline/spec at that moment.

  Example:
  - `Mercenary / Bodyguard`

- `ApplyEffect` for passive or login buffs
  Non-combat logs often show buffs being applied when the player loads in,
  changes area, or receives passive bonuses.

  Common examples from your sample:
  - `Major Experience Boost`
  - `Safe Login Immunity`
  - `Sprint`
  - `Hunter's Boon`
  - `Mark of Power`
  - `Unnatural Might`
  - `Coordination`

- `RemoveEffect`
  Shows non-combat effects ending.

  Common example from your sample:
  - `Safe Login Immunity` being removed after load-in

- `Event: TargetSet`
  Shows that one entity selected another as a target.
  This does not mean combat started.
  It only means a target was selected.

- Companion state lines
  Companion buffs and passive effects can also appear in non-combat logs.
  These often happen when zoning, loading in, or when group/passive buffs
  are mirrored onto the companion.


## What This Non-Combat Sample Tells Us

From your example file, we can tell that:

- The player moved between `Imperial Fleet` and `Dromund Kaas`.
- The player’s discipline was logged as `Mercenary / Bodyguard`.
- Login or transition-related buffs were applied and later removed.
- Group or passive buffs were applied to both the player and companion.
- Another player targeted you at one point.
- No real combat occurred because there are no `EnterCombat`, `ExitCombat`,
  `Damage`, or `Heal` lines tied to a fight.


## Suggested Non-Combat Categories

If you want to parse these logs later, a useful starting classification is:

- `Location`
  Example: `AreaEntered`

- `Character State`
  Example: `DisciplineChanged`

- `Buff Applied`
  Example: `ApplyEffect` for passive or login buffs

- `Buff Removed`
  Example: `RemoveEffect`

- `Targeting`
  Example: `TargetSet`, `TargetCleared`

- `Companion Sync`
  Buffs or state updates applied to the companion during travel or loading


## Targeting State Events

The log can also record targeting behavior even when no combat happens.

- `TargetSet`
  Means the source entity selected the target entity.
  This does not guarantee an attack happened.
  It only tells you that targeting occurred.

  Example meaning:
  - `Player A targeted Player B`
  - `NPC targeted you`

- `TargetCleared`
  Means the source entity cleared its current target.

These events are useful for:

- seeing when your character was targeted
- tracking target swaps
- understanding pre-combat behavior
- understanding boss or mob targeting patterns

Important limitation:

- `TargetSet` means target selection, not damage.
- You usually need later combat or ability lines to know whether the target was actually attacked.
